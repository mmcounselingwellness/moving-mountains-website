Meet Angela

Angela O'Neil, LICSW

Licensed Independent Clinical Social Worker/Therapist, MSW, LICSW

Accepting new clients

Feeling overwhelmed by anxiety, depression, stress, grief, or relationship challenges? Many people reach out to therapy when life begins to feel heavier than expected. You may find yourself constantly worrying, emotionally exhausted from work stress or burnout, struggling with low mood, or feeling stuck in patterns that leave you frustrated or disconnected. Others may be navigating pregnancy or postpartum changes, grief and loss, trauma, or major life transitions that bring uncertainty and emotional strain. Therapy can provide a space to slow down, make sense of what you're experiencing, and begin moving forward with support.

I work with adults navigating anxiety, depression, bipolar disorder, trauma, grief, chronic stress, relationship challenges, and major life transitions. I also support individuals experiencing pregnancy and postpartum adjustments, as well as those struggling with anger, self-esteem, or feeling overwhelmed by responsibilities at work or at home. Many of the people I work with are thoughtful, capable individuals who are used to carrying a lot on their own but are ready for a space where they can reflect, gain clarity, and develop healthier ways of coping.

My approach to therapy is collaborative, compassionate, and tailored to each person’s unique experiences. Together we work to understand the deeper roots of emotional distress while also building practical tools to manage anxiety, improve mood, and navigate difficult situations with greater confidence. I integrate Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy (MBCT), Internal Family Systems (IFS), relational and family systems perspectives, narrative therapy, motivational interviewing, and psychoanalytic insight to help clients better understand themselves and create meaningful change.

I believe that each person already carries important strengths within them-even if those strengths feel difficult to access during periods of anxiety, depression, stress, or grief. My role is to partner with you in identifying patterns, understanding the root causes of challenges, and developing healthier ways to cope and move forward.

If you're struggling with anxiety, depression, bipolar disorder, grief, trauma, relationship stress, pregnancy or postpartum changes, or major life transitions, you don’t have to navigate it alone. Therapy can help you gain clarity, build resilience, and begin creating lasting change. If this resonates with you, I would welcome the opportunity to connect.
