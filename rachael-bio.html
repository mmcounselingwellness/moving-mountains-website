Meet Rachael

Rachael Bottone, LMHC

Licensed Mental Health Counselor

Accepting new clients

Rachael works with teenagers (13+) and adults navigating anxiety, depression, life transitions, self-esteem struggles, trauma, relationship stress, body image concerns, disordered eating patterns, personality disorders, and the exhausting voice of the inner critic. She also has a strong interest in supporting first responders and individuals who often feel pressure to "hold it together" for everyone else.

Her approach to therapy is compassionate, honest, and grounded in genuine human connection. She believes healing happens when people feel safe enough to be fully themselves — while also being supported, challenged, and held accountable in meaningful ways. Many of her clients are people who feel deeply misunderstood, emotionally overwhelmed, self-critical, or disconnected from themselves and others. Her goal is to help clients feel heard, valued, and more connected to who they are beneath the stress, shame, anxiety, or self-doubt.

Rachael uses a trauma-informed and evidence-based approach, integrating DBT (Dialectical Behavior Therapy), self-compassion work, emotional regulation skills, and internal dialogue exploration to help clients better understand themselves and create lasting change. She is also trained in the Flash Technique, a trauma treatment approach designed to help reduce the intensity and emotional impact of distressing memories in a gentler and more accessible way.

In addition to her clinical work, she is a certified personal trainer with a nutrition component and values a holistic view of wellness and mental health. She believes the mind and body are deeply connected and enjoys helping clients explore healing through movement, mindfulness, and self-care practices that feel realistic and sustainable. Her personal interests in yoga, meditation, nature, water, and the outdoors naturally influence her therapeutic style and perspective.

As part of Moving Mountains Counseling & Wellness, she offers in-office, virtual, and walk-and-talk therapy sessions for clients interested in incorporating nature and movement into the healing process. She looks forward to supporting clients both inside and outside the therapy office — creating space for growth, reflection, and meaningful change.
